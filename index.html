000 Result. Given a prime number $p$, the number of solutions to the equation $1+x^2=y^2$ with $x,y\in\Z/p$ is $p-1$.
000 Guidance. Use the factorization $y^2-x^2=(y-x)(y+x)$.
000 Explanation. The bijection $(x,y)\mapsto (y-x,y+x)$ of $(\Z/p)^2$ allows us to count instead the number of solutions to $ab=1$ with $a,b\in\Z/p$, which is of course $p-1$.
